There's a particular kind of terror in publishing a blank page to the void. Not because the page itself is empty — it isn't, not anymore — but because it represents a commitment. A promise that you'll show up again. That you'll have something worth saying. That this little corner of the internet won't join the graveyard of abandoned blogs and forgotten side projects.

I've started blogs before. Good ones, even. They lasted three, maybe four posts before life crept in and the momentum faded. The familiar cycle: enthusiasm, procrastination, guilt, abandonment. So why am I doing this again?

The answer is that this time, I'm not building a blog. I'm building a garden.

A blog is linear. Posts appear in reverse chronological order, and after a week, they're buried. A garden is different. It grows organically. Ideas cross-pollinate. Some plants bloom immediately; others take seasons. You tend to it not because you have to, but because the act of tending is itself the point.

## Why "Aura"

The name came to me during a late-night debugging session. I'd been staring at my screen for hours, tweaking the blur values on a glass panel, watching light refract through translucent layers. There was something almost ethereal about it — this quality of light that couldn't quite be pinned down, that shifted depending on where you stood.

An aura is exactly that: a luminous glow surrounding something, felt more than seen. It's the atmosphere that makes you feel something before you understand why. That's what I want this space to be — a place where the feeling arrives first, and the meaning follows.

## What You'll Find Here

This isn't a tutorial blog. You won't find "10 Tips for Better React Code" or "How I Optimized My Lighthouse Score." Those posts have their place, but this isn't it.

Instead, expect writing about the *why* behind the work. Why glassmorphism isn't just a trend. Why spring physics make interfaces feel alive. Why minimalism in code mirrors minimalism in life. Why the best design decision is often the one you don't make.

I'll also share projects as they happen — half-formed ideas, experiments that failed, things that surprised me. The goal is transparency, not curation.

## The Technical Stack

For the curious, this site is built with:

- **Next.js 16** with the App Router and React 19
- **Tailwind CSS 4** for styling
- **Framer Motion** for spring-based animations
- **MDX** for content
- **Lenis** for buttery smooth scrolling

Every design decision was made with intention. The glass panels use real `backdrop-blur` with subtle gradient borders. The aurora background isn't a video or image — it's pure CSS with animated radial gradients. The typography pairs Playfair Display (serif) with Geist Sans for contrast between warmth and precision.

I'll write more about the technical decisions in future posts. For now, this is enough.

## What This Represents

More than anything, this garden is an exercise in consistency. It's easy to build something beautiful once. The hard part is maintaining it. Iterating on it. Letting it evolve without losing its soul.

So here's to showing up. Here's to the first post, and to all the ones that will follow.

Welcome to Aura. ✨
